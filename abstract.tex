\textbf{Abstract}
\end{center}
Blockchain education typically relies on theoretical lectures, often missing the technical depth required for real-world implementation. This thesis presents a modular Educational Blockchain Cryptocurrency System and an accompanying laboratory curriculum designed to bridge this gap in university computer science programs. We develop a Python-based prototype that guides students from network fundamentals to the implementation of consensus mechanisms, specifically Proof of Work and Proof of Stake, while benchmarking critical metrics like energy consumption. To demonstrate system extensibility beyond financial applications, the curriculum includes multiple educational scenarios and real-life use cases on adapting blockchain systems, such as a Library Management System. Furthermore, the practical component is enhanced by a Vue.js Blockchain Explorer, which provides real-time visualization of the blockchain network operations, such as block propagation and mempool status. The resulting ecosystem offers a robust dual solution: a technical prototype and a structured educational framework that effectively strengthens practical knowledge of distributed ledger technologies.\vspace{0.3cm}

\textbf{Keywords:} Educational Blockchain, Cryptocurrency Prototype, Laboratory Curriculum, Consensus Algorithms, Proof of Stake, Library Management System, Blockchain Explorer, Python.
